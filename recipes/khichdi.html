Khichdi for Tummy Aches

A gentle, healing rice and lentil dish that's perfect when you're feeling under the weather or need something soothing for your digestive system.

Why Khichdi?

Khichdi is a traditional Indian dish that combines rice and lentils into a simple, easy-to-digest meal. In Ayurvedic medicine, it's considered a healing food that balances all three doshas. The simple ingredients and mild spices make it perfect for sensitive stomachs while still providing complete protein and nutrients.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup white rice (basmati works well)
  • 1/4 cup yellow moong dal (split mung beans)
  • 1 tbsp oil or vegan ghee
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric
  • 1/4 tsp ginger powder (or 1/2 inch fresh ginger, grated)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2-3 cups water (depending on desired consistency)
  • Optional: 1/2 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas) if tolerated

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice and dal together until the water runs clear
  2. In a pot or pressure cooker, heat the oil or vegan ghee
  3. Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle for 30 seconds
  4. Add turmeric and ginger, stir for a few seconds
  5. Add the rinsed rice and dal mixture, stir to coat with spices
  6. Add water and salt
  7. If using a pressure cooker: Cook for 2-3 whistles, then let pressure release naturally
  8. If using a pot: Bring to a boil, then simmer covered for 20-25 minutes until soft and porridge-like
  9. For extra digestive comfort, mash slightly before serving

Serving Suggestions

Serve plain when stomach is very upset, or with a squeeze of lemon juice and a small spoonful of vegan yogurt when feeling a bit better. The consistency can be adjusted from thick to soupy depending on what feels best for your digestion.

Storage

Khichdi will keep in the refrigerator for 2-3 days. Add a splash of water when reheating as it tends to thicken.
