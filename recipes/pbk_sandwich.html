PB&K (Kimchi Peanut Butter Tofu Sandwich)

A unique fusion sandwich that combines the umami of kimchi with the richness of peanut butter and the protein of tofu.

About This Recipe

This might sound like an unusual combination, but the spicy, tangy kimchi pairs surprisingly well with creamy peanut butter, while the tofu adds protein and substance. It's a quick, satisfying meal that costs very little to make but delivers big on flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 slices of bread (any kind you prefer)
  • 2 tbsp natural peanut butter
  • 1/4 cup vegan kimchi, drained and chopped
  • 3-4 slices of firm tofu (about 1/2 inch thick)
  • 1 tsp soy sauce or tamari
  • 1 tsp maple syrup or agave (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp sriracha or hot sauce (optional, if you want extra heat)
  • Fresh cilantro or green onions (optional)

Instructions

  1. Press the tofu slices between paper towels to remove excess moisture
  2. In a small bowl, mix soy sauce and maple syrup (if using)
  3. Heat a small pan over medium heat and lightly pan-fry the tofu slices until golden, about 2-3 minutes per side
  4. Brush the tofu with the soy sauce mixture and cook for another 30 seconds until it caramelizes slightly
  5. Toast your bread slices if desired
  6. Spread peanut butter on one slice of bread
  7. Layer the pan-fried tofu on top of the peanut butter
  8. Add the chopped kimchi on top of the tofu
  9. If using, add sriracha and sprinkle with cilantro or green onions
  10. Top with the second slice of bread and enjoy!

Variations

  • Extra Crunch: Add thinly sliced cucumber or lettuce
  • Avocado Version: Replace half the peanut butter with mashed avocado
  • Wrap Version: Use all ingredients in a tortilla wrap instead of bread

Budget Tips

Make your own kimchi when cabbage is in season to save money. Tofu is often cheaper at Asian grocery stores. This sandwich uses very little tofu per serving, making one block stretch across many meals.
